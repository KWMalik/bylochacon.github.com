A while back I needed to distill the main points of this book to present in a course I was taking - I thought I’d share them here. (To download these notes as a pdf, click here:  World Trade Created Notes Organizer.pdf)


Pomeranz, Kenneth, and Steven Topik. The World That Trade Created: Society, Culture, and the World Economy, 1400 to the Present. Armonk, NY: M.E. Sharpe, 2006.


1.  Through the Creation of Market Conventions

  1. -Economic activities are social acts that bring groups with very different ideas together.

  2. -Intensification of trade in this period led to increasing agreements on trade conventions.

  3. -Trade diasporas organized trade until colonialism.


2.  Transport Tactics

  1. -Transport costs limited the size of cities.

  2. -The railroad and steamships brought the world closer together and elevated manufacturers and financiers over merchants.

  3. -Technology allowed people to keep in touch with home, and ended the necessity of integration.


3.  Economic Culture of Drugs

  1. -What has changed over time is not the value, but the definition of drug.

  2. -Governments eventually legalized many so they could tax them.

  3. -Colonial empires were built on the foundation of drug trades.

  4. -Drugs have been both the foundation and bane of states.


4.  Commodities in World Trade

  1. -As specific commodities became global, their values and meanings changed from what they had been locally.

  2. -New global commodities often took on associations antithetical to their natural properties.

  3. -Consuming societies tried to synthesize replacements and/or transplant them.


5.  Economics of Violence

  1. -Relationships between violence and entrepreneurship have always existed.

  2. -Warfare and economic growth are often not contradictory.

  3. -Warfare has often been the mother of invention.

  4. -Financing war without participating is also quite profitable.


6.  Making Modern Markets

  1. -For there to be a true world economy, laws and practices have to be predictable and universal.

  2. -Standardization & impersonal relations were the key.

  3. -Gold and silver became the money standards.

  4. -Industrialization has caused people to trust distant logos more than local production.


7.  World Trade, Industrialization & De-Industrialization

  1. -Very few countries have instituted free trade.

  2. -Industrialization in a society depends on a sharp increase in trade.

  3. -Because industrialization coincided with imperialism, we can never know how much industry-increased trade was lost.
