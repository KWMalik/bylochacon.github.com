“If you’re unfamiliar with Nings, they’re like a specialized version of Facebook, so rather than assaulting you with all the news all the time about your “Friends,” the English Companion Ning seeks to help  English and Language Arts teachers help one another online. You can join a group (there’s one on teaching about the Holocaust), share ideas, and participate in online discussions. This Ning is easy to join and currently has over 20,000 members (more than 10% of secondary level LA and English teachers nationwide).” -- Pete Fredlake, USHMM

English Companion Ning

http://englishcompanion.ning.com

This isn’t world history directly, but they have workshops on the history of the soviet union and U.S. economic history.  They teach teachers the information first, something often lacking in economic education.  The lessons they demonstrate and give you are practical, engaging, and extremely effective.  The conferences are held in fun places, and are small enough groups that you are involved in the activity, and not simply a member of the crowd.  There are resources online and helpful emailing lists.

FOUNDATION FOR TEACHING ECONOMICS

http://www.fte.org

The USHMM local conference was my first experience with the museum, and within three years, I was a teaching fellow.  This program treats teachers with respect, as professionals.  It focuses on how to teach difficult material, but also provides teachers with the background information they need to be able to teach it.  There is almost limitless help through the fellows, regional coordinators and museum staff.  The website is among the best I’ve ever used, of any type.

STUDENT’S FRIEND

This organization is one of the best teacher-training experiences I have had.  Great strategies demonstrated, wonderful materials, a year-long cumulative project you can be part of or not, support all year long, and truly focused on teaching world history.  This program had effective results in my own classroom.  Students of all levels and attitudes were engaged, producing and learning.
