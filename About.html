ABOUT ME

 
 

I taught high school world history for eight years in San Francisco Bay Area, have a Master of Arts in World History with an emphasis in teaching from San José State University, and a Bachelor of Arts in History from the University of California, Santa Barbara.  I was selected as a teaching fellow at the United States Holocaust Memorial Museum for 2007-2008.  To learn more about genocide and its related issues, I have traveled to Poland, the Czech Republic, Kenya and Rwanda.  Currently, I am studying, working on the board of Running Chicken (formerly Globaleyes), and taking care of my little girl.  My longer-term goal is to enter a PhD program when my daughter is older in order to further my studies.  For now, I want to continue in education by helping teachers.
