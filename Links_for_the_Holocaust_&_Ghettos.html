German invasion of Poland in September 1939 set off a series of events that forever altered the Jewish community of Lodz, Poland, one of the largest in Europe. Despite the Germans’ violent oppression and imposed squalor and hunger, the community leadership strove to create a “normal” world for the children as long as it could. Lodz Ghetto

http://www.holocaustresearchproject.org/ghettos/Lodz/lodzghetto.html

Spiritual resistance refers to attempts by individuals to maintain humanity and dignity in the face of efforts to degrade them. During the Holocaust, countless Jews—in ghettos, concentration camps, or in hiding—engaged in resistance by refusing to allow their spirit to be broken even under profoundly dehumanizing circumstances. The “camp-ghetto” of Theresienstadt offered perhaps the most favorable environment for spiritual resistance in the Nazi camp system. (from site)
